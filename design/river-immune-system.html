River's Immune System

A defense engine that keeps River actually working, not just running. River's current self-healer (PR #99) is an excellent crash detector — but in 24 catalogued production incidents, crashes accounted for zero of them. The pain is entirely in failures where River was up, healthy, and wrong: 8 days silently on the metered API, deep_search hollow for 11 commits, a 33-minute invisible Signal socket during a live meetup, a wrong calendar URL for weeks. This doc designs the missing sense organ: a ProbeRegistry of deterministic antibodies (each past scar becomes a permanent probe for that shape of failure), a boot layer that degrades loud rather than failing quiet, and — gated hard behind the existing #110 safety rails plus the new controls below — an agentic remediation layer where River diagnoses and repairs from an allowlisted action set with bounded-provenance values.

Scope of the claim (corrected after cage-match): this is immune memory, not general immunity. A deterministic probe makes a known shape of silent-semantic failure visible; it does not catch novel semantic failures (wrong-ranking, stale-corpus, wrong-tenant, calendar-wrong-but-nonempty, delivery-to-wrong-room). Those are enumerated as a coverage taxonomy, not claimed as covered.

Honest status: nothing here is built. This is a design grounded in two evidence artifacts (a 24-incident failure catalogue and a full current-coverage map, both mined this session from the repo + memory graph). The incident catalogue is archaeology — durations and root causes are best-effort, not audited — so the backtest gates on synthetic fault injection + healthy-baseline runs, not history replay alone. The agentic-remediation layer is a real trust boundary + blast-radius surface and its safety gates are a merge prerequisite, not a follow-up. Build order is forced: you cannot remediate what you cannot sense, so the sensing spine ships and is backtested (positively and negatively) before River is given hands.
Cage-match revision log (2026-07-08, PR #118). This doc was adversarially reviewed by four model families before any code: Maxwell (Claude, COMMENT), Kelvin (Gemini, APPROVE), Carnot (GPT, REQUEST_CHANGES), Tesla (Grok, REQUEST_CHANGES). Every finding is folded in below; the changes cluster around one principle (the observation–actuation impedance match). Headline changes: Layer 0 now degrades rather than bricks (§L0); the diagnosis-context trust boundary is pinned to structured, non-string inputs (§L2); a global thrash-breaker + energy budget join the per-probe breaker; probes carry a read-only-by-default side-effect contract; the self-check tick is exempted from the crash-health signal so the immune system can't trip PR #99's restart arc; and the backtest adds negative fixtures as an equal merge gate.

Round 2 (same PR) re-ran the four adversaries against the revision. They graded 7 of 8 round-1 findings genuinely fixed and surfaced deeper gaps that only became visible once the shallow ones closed. Folded in here: probe measurement-integrity — sentinels must be structurally unreachable from any user-writable store, and invariants assert identity (UID/ETag/tenant), not mere non-emptiness (§probe integrity); a first-class maintenance mode resolving the OAuth-off-boot contradiction (§L0); and explicit health-surface partitioning (/ready vs /health vs /immune) making the crash-arc isolation concrete (§health surfaces). The remaining round-2 items are captured as build-time acceptance criteria, not silently deferred.

Purpose

The Night-Loop Spine opens by declaring itself the offense engine and dismissing "watch, detect, audit, reconcile" as mere defense. This is that defense — and it turns out defense is not a solved problem River can take for granted. River breaks in production often, and the breaks that hurt are precisely the ones his current healer cannot see. The two engines are the poles of one nervous system: the night-loop manufactures what should exist; the immune system keeps what exists alive. They can eventually share a substrate — an antibody is a "standing fire" that glows when its probe trips — but they must not share a single failure-coupled tick (see shared-cavity risk).

Who/what it is for: River (the dreamfinder bot) in production on OCI, plus its bridge mesh. The beneficiary of a working immune system is Nick — it removes his current job of being River's failure detector (he found the metered drift, the hollow deep_search, and the empty calendar by hand). This is design-for-subtraction: the human stops being the monitor — provided the system does not turn him into its false-positive filter instead (see probe specificity).

The organising principle: observation–actuation impedance match

The four reviewers independently circled one shape. Carnot named it: "the danger is the gradient between uncertain observations and real-world actuation, and that gradient is not yet safely constrained." Every serious finding is an instance:

An action's power must be constrained to match the certainty of the observation driving it. A boot-assert (a blocking action) fired on an uncertain semantic read bricks the host. A repointConfig (an irreversible write) fired on a possibly-poisoned sentinel manufactures confident drift. An auto-revendor (a high-mutation act) fired on a flaky probe thrashes the artifact bare. The immune system itself (overhead) read as "unhealthy" trips the crash arc. The whole design is now organised so that uncertain reads may only degrade — never block or actuate — and every write carries bounded provenance, reversibility, and a budget.

Architecture: three layers + an immune memory

Autonomic reflexes are deterministic and always-on; conscious self-examination is periodic and agentic. Detection is never agentic (an LLM sensor can be fooled by River's own hallucination — or by a poisoned corpus); only diagnosis and repair are, and those run on structured inputs, never raw content.

Layer 0 — Autonomic reflexes (boot + always-on): degrade loud, don't fail quiet — and don't fail-to-boot

The Docker-healthcheck→restart arc stays. Boot checks are split into two tiers, because "fail loud" must not mean "fail to start" — a semantic probe that hard-blocks readiness turns a legitimately-empty calendar or a quiet week into a self-inflicted total outage (trading 8-day-silent-wrong for 8-day-silent-dead).

TierChecksOn violation
Hard invariant (local, deterministic, no external dependency)auth mode resolves to OAuth or an explicit MaintenanceMode is set; required env vars present & well-formed; DB opens at expected schemawithhold readiness — these are true prerequisites to serving correctly
Degraded-readiness (remote / semantic / dependency-bound)calendar.events > 0; deep_search non-hollow; RAG retrieval non-emptystart anyway, mark the capability degraded in /health, escalate once — never block boot
Distinguish misconfiguration from legitimate emptiness. An empty calendar might be a wrong URL (bad) or a quiet week (fine); Layer 0 cannot tell, so it must degrade the calendar capability and alert, not refuse to run. Only checks that are certainly a misconfiguration when they fail (auth mode, env presence) are allowed to gate startup.

Three health surfaces, three consumers (making crash-arc isolation concrete)

The first revision claimed the self-check tick is "isolated from the crash signal" but still reported semantic degradation to /health — a contradiction (Carnot r2 #1): if Docker's healthcheck consumes /health, a degraded there still fires PR #99's restart arc. Resolved by three distinct endpoints with named consumers:

EndpointReportsConsumed by
/readyhard invariants only (auth-or-maintenance, env, DB)readiness gate — refuses traffic until true
/healthcrash liveness only (poll-stale, processing-stuck) — never semantic or immune-tick stateDocker healthcheck → PR #99 restart arc

The immune system reports its own health on /immune, watched by something with no authority to restart the container — so a slow or degraded probe can never trip the crash arc. Corollary (Maxwell r2): a wedged self-check tick must still be caught — /immune carries the immune system's own liveness on a separate watchdog timer, so removing it from /health does not create a silent-deadlock blind spot.

Layer 1 — Behavioral senses (deterministic probing — the missing organ)

A ProbeRegistry: a declarative library where each entry carries a probe, an invariant, a side-effect class, a coverage tag, a healthy-baseline fixture, and a Layer-2 remediation hook.

Probe {
  id:            "deep_search_lore",
  invocation:    () => deepSearch(FIXTURE_QUERY),   // fixed test query, not live user text
  invariant:     (r) => r.contains(KNOWN_SENTINEL), // deterministic assert, NO LLM
  sideEffect:    SideEffect.pureRead,               // see contract below
  covers:        [Failure.hollow],                  // NOT stale/wrong-tenant/wrong-recipient
  healthyBaseline: FIXTURE_HEALTHY_CORPUS,          // must NOT trip on this — specificity gate
  failureClass:  "integration-hollow",
  remediation:   Action.reVendorClis,
  autonomy:      AutonomyLevel.detectOnly,          // per-probe; promoted only after PR3 sign-off
  timeoutMs:     3000, costCeiling: 0,              // bounded; a slow probe degrades to "unknown"
}

The probe contract (Tesla + Carnot: "the antibody must not become the pathogen")

  • Read-only by default. Every probe declares a SideEffect ∈ {pureRead, idempotentWrite, externalSend, paidCall, cacheMutating}. Anything past pureRead requires an explicit sandbox target (a shadow room, a fixture doc, a dry-run flag) and Nick sign-off — a heartbeat probe must never append sentinels to the real Outline, spend metered credits, perturb messages_processed, or send over a live bridge.
  • Bounded. Hard per-probe timeout and a cost ceiling; a probe that exceeds either degrades to unknown (not failed) and never blocks the tick.
  • Specificity is a first-class gate, not an afterthought. Every probe ships with a healthy-baseline fixture it must not trip on. The messages_processed-delta antibody in particular must define a legitimate-quiet steady state (night/holiday/demo-ended) or it converts Nick back into the human false-positive filter.

Per-probe coverage taxonomy (what "visible" actually means)

A sentinel probe proves one failure mode is absent. Each probe tags which of these it covers, and — per the instrument-not-blind rule — which it is blind to, logged as future antibodies:

hollow · stale · wrong-tenant · wrong-recipient · incomplete · delayed · side-effect · wrong-but-nonempty

Example: deep_search_lore covers hollow; it is blind to stale (old corpus), wrong-tenant (auth scoped wrong), and wrong-but-nonempty (a plausible wrong answer). Those blind spots are named, not hidden — this is why the headline claim is immune memory, not general immunity.

Probe measurement-integrity — the deepest round-2 finding

Round 2's best catch (Tesla r2 #1, Carnot r2 #2/#4): round 1 purified the diagnosis inputs (structured-only) but left the probes themselves reading the same poisoned corpus the incidents describe. Cleaning the doctor's ears does nothing if the stethoscope is bugged. An attacker who plants the sentinel string in lore makes deep_search_lore pass while the tool is hollow on every real query; one who strips it forces a false failure and a remediation. So two rules are now merge-grade:

  • Sentinels are structurally unreachable from any user-writable store. A probe's expected answer lives in a fixture-isolated index, an HMAC-sealed golden doc, or an out-of-band file — never "a string we hope stays rare" in the same corpus users and bridges write to. If the corpus can contain the sentinel, the corpus can forge the reading.
  • Invariants assert identity, not non-emptiness. events > 0 is too weak — a wrong-tenant or obsolete calendar satisfies it (Carnot: "the sink cannot tell the heat came from the right furnace merely by observing that it is warm"). Probe a pinned fixture UID / expected calendar ETag / account-path metadata, so the invariant distinguishes right from merely non-empty.
  • Post-action verification must be stronger than the triggering probe. Re-running the same blind probe can certify a narrow sentinel while a broader semantic failure (wrong tenant, obsolete path) persists — healer-induced drift wearing a green check. Write remediations verify with an identity/tenant check whose coverage exceeds the probe that fired them.
  • Honest health = failure rate, not last-success. A probe asks "what fraction is hollow right now?", not "did the last call eventually succeed?" (incident #8's blind spot).
  • Config truth is scoped by node. River's own config is the in-process env — sufficient to catch the metered drift (River reads its own auth mode). Other containers' state (mautrix-signal) needs the constrained side-car (see Risks); Layer 0/1 never assert on a node they can't authentically read, to avoid "escalate healthy prod into remediated wrong."

Layer 2 — Agentic diagnosis + remediation (River as his own doctor), on a pinned trust boundary

When a probe trips, River wakes a bounded self-heal session and picks a remediation from an allowlisted action set. Two things are now pinned that the first draft left porous:

The diagnosis context is structured-only (Carnot + Kelvin + Tesla: injection needs no user text, only contaminated truth)

The first draft contradicted itself ("failing probe plus context" vs "only deterministic probe results"). Resolved: the diagnosis context is a whitelist of structured, non-string fields — probe id (enum), failureClass (enum), invariant expected-vs-actual as booleans/enums/counts, and timestamps. No free text from tool outputs, RAG store, calendar payloads, room names, bridge metadata, or logs enters the prompt. This matters because probe outputs traverse the same poisoned corpus the incidents describe: an attacker who writes lore or a calendar title could otherwise own the sentinel string and, through it, steer which allowlisted hand fires.

The action set — and why the allowlist is not enough on its own

enum Action {
  reVendorClis,               // re-run vendor script + re-verify manifest; NOT auto until PR3
  repointConfig(key, value),  // key AND value both from a precomputed last-known-good map
  restartConnection(name),    // named bridge/MCP reconnect (in-process, low blast radius)
  clearCorruptHistory,        // already exists in the sync loop
  restartContainer(name),     // ⚠ side-car only — never in-process Docker socket; see Risks
  escalateOnly,               // fail-closed default for anything unrecognised
}

Bounded keys do not bound values. repointConfig can redirect auth, calendars, room allowlists, or webhooks — a "friendly name" over a high-power write. So its value comes from a precomputed desired-state map (a checked-in key → known-good-value table), never a value the LLM infers. That map is itself a security object, not just a config file (Carnot/Kelvin r2): it is environment-bound (refuses to apply if the running env doesn't match), owned/reviewed, monotonically versioned, and integrity-checked (deploy-time hash/signature) — otherwise it becomes healer-induced drift with a pedigree, "yesterday's truth applied with confidence." A stale map is a bad value with a certificate. Every write action is reversible: pre-action snapshot → act → post-action verify (re-run the probe) → auto-rollback + escalate if the probe still fails. A mis-diagnosed repoint that lands a wrong-but-valid URL would otherwise create a new class — healer-induced drift, worse than the original because it arrives with notify-backstop confidence.

Budget, cooldown, and a global thrash-breaker (Tesla: the breaker must guard the chord, not one frequency)

The #110 rails (single-action CAS latch, notify-backstop, per-probe circuit-breaker) are reused as the policy layer, extended with the controls infra-remediation needs:

  • Global energy budget — a max number of remediations per rolling window across all probes, a per-action cooldown, exponential backoff, and a hard max-restarts/day. One upstream outage trips many antibodies; without a global cap they'd drain the action budget in a burst.
  • Correlation suppression is deterministic, never LLM-inferred — probes sharing a root cause (auth down → deep_search + calendar + RAG all trip) are grouped by a hand-authored, versioned dependsOn tag on the Probe rows (or an upstream-health singleton), so the healer acts once on the cause. If the dependency is ambiguous, it fails closed to per-probe handling — an LLM grouping symptoms would re-open actuation on uncertain inference through a side door (Carnot/Tesla r2).
  • Global thrash-breaker — beyond the per-probe "N failed self-heals → pause", a cross-probe breaker halts all autonomous action when the aggregate heal-rate crosses a threshold, because reVendorClis fixing deep_search can break the auth probe, and six remediations queued behind one CAS latch each perturb the substrate the others measure.
  • Restart-durable breaker state. The breaker counters persist in bot_metadata and are read before the first post-boot heal — because remediation includes restarts, an in-memory counter would reset every crash-loop cycle and thrash forever.
  • Fail-closed on the unknown — an unrecognised failure or out-of-allowlist fix resolves to escalateOnly.

The immune memory (why it compounds — and how it avoids autoimmunity)

Because the antibody library is declarative, every future break becomes a one-row addition: new scar → new Probe → River never takes that shape of wound silently again. It is a regression suite for production reality that lives inside River. For a golem who inspects his own inscription, immune memory is the data structure.

But monotonic growth is a liability without a lifecycle (Kelvin: "an immune system that attacks healthy tissue is autoimmune disease"; Tesla: the registry becomes "a growing shunt load"). Every probe therefore carries: an owner, a sentinel version pinned to the data it depends on (so a corpus change retires the sentinel, not fires it), an expiry / recalibration date, and a flake-quarantine path (a probe with a rising false-alarm rate is auto-parked and flagged, not left to fill /health with degraded).

The backtest (falsifier) — positive and negative, both as merge gates

Replaying the 24 incidents proves sensitivity on archaeology — and "a backtest over imprecise memories can certify folklore" (Carnot). So the gate is two-sided and equal:

Intended invariants (once built)

Relabelled from "Guarantees (as built)" — nothing is built; these are the properties the implementation must hold.

  • Detection is deterministic; only diagnosis/repair is agentic, and the agentic context is structured-only (no attacker-influenceable strings).
  • Uncertain reads may only degrade, never block or actuate (the impedance match). Boot never fails on a semantic check.
  • Every write action is bounded-provenance + reversible + budgeted: allowlisted key and value, pre-snapshot, post-verify, auto-rollback, under a global energy budget with a cross-probe thrash-breaker.
  • The immune system cannot invoke the crash arc. The self-check tick is isolated from the PR #99 health signal (see below) so its own overhead never reads as degraded.
  • Sensing ships and is backtested — positively and negatively — before hands are wired.

Two coupling risks the reviewers surfaced

  • High — the self-restart feedback loop (Tesla). The self-check tick shares River's process and /health surface with PR #99's crash healer. A slow or piled-up tick can push /health to degraded → Docker restarts the container → the defense system invoked the offense's kill switch on itself. Fix: run probes off the health-critical path with hard timeouts, and explicitly exclude self-check latency/failure from the poll-stale / processing-stuck signals. The immune system's health is reported on a separate channel that never feeds the restart reflex.
  • High — offense/defense on one coil (Tesla). Riding the #16 heartbeat means a slow probe can delay or starve the night-loop payload on the same tick. Fix: the heartbeat host must partition the two payloads with backpressure — a stalled probe cannot consume the offense budget, and vice-versa. Until #16 exists, the interim 60s-scheduler host gets the same per-job hard-timeout (the coverage scout found it has none today).

Designed, not built

Status: the entire document is a design. No probe, registry, boot assertion, remediation action, budget, or backtest harness exists yet. The evidence base is two scout reports produced this session; the incident catalogue is archaeology.
  • The ProbeRegistry & every antibody — concept only; sentinel queries, invariants, timeouts, and healthy baselines unwritten.
  • The two-tier boot layer — the split is designed; the exact hard-invariant set is TBD.
  • The allowlisted action set + last-known-good value map + reversibility harness — named, not implemented. restartContainer has an unsolved capability question (below).
  • #110-rails compatibility for infra remediation — the rails were built for Community Spark; a compatibility analysis (what resource is latched, where CAS state lives, restart-idempotency, notify-backstop-failure behaviour) is required before reuse is asserted, not assumed.

Risks & Limitations

  • High (BLOCKING for autonomy) — Agent with world-acting hands on a partly-poisoned corpus. River's inputs and its probe outputs both traverse an attacker-influenceable relay mesh + lore store. Mitigation is a merge prerequisite: structured-only diagnosis context, closed action allowlist, last-known-good values, reversibility, and notify-backstop on every act. Cage-match by law.
  • High (BLOCKING for restartContainer) — No safe container-restart primitive today. Mounting the Docker socket into River's container is a container-escape-equivalent and an automatic reject (Kelvin). Options: a constrained side-car accepting only a fixed restart <named-container> set over an authenticated local channel, or leaving container-restart escalateOnly permanently. Until resolved, #6's auto-heal stays detect-and-escalate.
  • High — Healer-induced drift. A confident wrong remediation (mis-diagnosed repoint) is worse than the original failure. Guarded by last-known-good values + post-action verify + auto-rollback; every act is logged/notified — never silent.
  • High — The immune system tripping the crash arc / starving the night-loop. See coupling risks; both are merge prerequisites, not follow-ups.
  • Med — Probe side-effects. Guarded by the read-only-by-default side-effect contract + sandbox targets for any write/send/paid probe + a global kill-switch for the whole tick.
  • Med — Sentinel/antibody rot. Guarded by the lifecycle (versioned sentinels, expiry, flake-quarantine, ownership).
  • Med — Backtest over archaeology. Guarded by the negative-fixture + fault-injection gates; the histogram shape is robust even where individual rows are imprecise.
  • Med — Alert storms. Escalation carries dedupe windows, severity mapping, dependency grouping, and explicit states (unknown/degraded/failed/recovering) — not a raw per-trip page.
  • Low — Coverage over-confidence. The per-probe taxonomy makes "what we do NOT cover" explicit so the antibody library is never mistaken for general immunity.
