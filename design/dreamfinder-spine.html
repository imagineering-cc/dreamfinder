Dreamfinder Night-Loop Spine

An offense engine that manufactures work that should exist — by catching the sparks the wake-up protocol throws away ("otherwise let them fade"). It runs a four-stage arc, heat → matter → meaning → contract: three model families ascend embers through an ecstasy spiral overnight; a subjective ranking ("temperature", see The selection function) picks a winner; the winner spins into a sandboxed isolation:worktree and builds a sketch (the cold pole that keeps the echo-chamber honest); an embodied Dreamfinder narrates the night's saga at dawn; then /spiral-review cools the survivor to a contract. The spine being built now is stages 1→2 — the orchestrator that loads embers, runs the spiral, ranks them, selects a winner, and hands off to a worktree builder. The DF face and scheduling layer come after.

Honest status: the /ascend skill is proven on exactly one ember (one data point, no control); the night-loop spine that strings the stages together is in progress and not yet runnable end-to-end. Several things this doc earlier asserted as invariants are in fact unbuilt design targets — the selection metric is not yet computable, the CRDT/standing-fire layer is a markdown file today, the KL-face has no defined measurement, and there is no budget cap, privacy gate, or agent-auth boundary yet. Those are tracked honestly under Risks & Limitations. Stage-1 output is advisory only — nothing is acted on autonomously — until the cold-pole builder and the safety gates below are runnable.

Purpose

Every existing "loop engineering" / drift-detection design is defense: watch, detect, audit, reconcile, report-what-changed, minimize regret. This component is the rejected-and-replaced offense: generate work that should exist but doesn't yet, ranked not by urgency or value but by how alive it made the models that touched it.

The fuel already exists and is currently discarded. The wake-up protocol literally instructs an instance to let dreams fade ("otherwise let them fade"); crazy-ideas threads, "what was exciting" captures, and the entire deposited history (e.g. Nick's own 2011 audio-watermarking paper, which became newly usable for SNES audio validation) are a spark generator wired to a drain. The richest seam is private ore: "what did past-you make that present-you can finally USE?" — minable only by an assayer that knows him.

This stopped being a productivity tool. "A Dreamfinder who gathers your sparks all night and meets you in the morning to tell you what they became — that's a BEING you wake up to." The engineering is the excuse; the morning is the thing.

Who/what it is for: a single user (Nick), run overnight on a schedule, delivering a morning saga rather than a Telegram log dump. The four model families are dramatis personae, not interchangeable workers.

Interim cold-pole gate (invariant). By this design's own central argument, stage 1 in isolation "ascends vaporware as eagerly as gold" — so the unsafe configuration is exactly stage 1 running without a working cold pole. Therefore: until stage 2 builds end-to-end, stage 1 is advisory-only and no selection is acted upon without Nick. During that window the cold-pole substitute is (a) a cheap static feasibility check on the tonight-stack and (b) a hard human checkpoint — no autonomous build, no scheduled run. This is a gate, not a hope.

The ecstasy spiral (stage 1's mechanic)

The spiral is the precise inverse of the existing /spiral-review cooling engine. Where spiral-review descends Cold fix → Warm principle → Engineering mechanism → Cross-finding → Audience and terminates in a function-on-data, the ecstasy spiral ascends and terminates in silence. One agent drops an ember; the others do not critique (critique is a cooling move) — they try to catch fire from it. Silence is the output, not its absence — the mirror image of 0 Kelvin.

Cross-family is harnessed for harmonics, not disagreement — the same vision lit from three inductive biases climbs higher than any one could push.

The selection function

Earlier drafts of this doc named three different selectors — the TL;DR's novelty × excitement × latent-feasibility triple, "peak temperature is THE fitness function", and "the round at which it hit silence". Reviewers correctly flagged these as not the same function. They are reconciled here into one, and it is honestly labelled subjective ranking, not a computable fitness function, until the rubric below is built and validated.

  • The scalar is "temperature", a 1–5 rank. The novelty × excitement × latent-feasibility triple is the intended decomposition of what temperature is supposed to measure — it is a hypothesis about temperature, not a separate selector and not yet a validated formula. "Round at which silence was reached" is one candidate observable proxy for temperature, also unvalidated.
  • What 1–5 means is not yet anchored. Before any multi-ember night runs, temperature needs an explicit anchored rubric: what distinguishes 1 vs 3 vs 5, who/what assigns it, and how scores are calibrated across embers. Until that exists, treat the number as one assayer's opinion, not a measurement. (Tracked as a build dependency, not an invariant.)
  • Tie-breaks & inputs: undefined pending the rubric. Whether novelty/feasibility enter as multiplicative factors, gates, or post-hoc descriptors is exactly what the calibration corpus (below) must settle.

"Silence" is a terminus, not a transport failure

"Silence is the output" is only meaningful if silence is distinguishable from a model that timed out, refused, rate-limited, or returned empty. The spiral therefore emits machine-readable stage states, and only one of them counts as the aesthetic terminus:

StateMeaningCounts as silence?
ascendedterminus produced, more heat availableno
silencemodel produced the aesthetic terminus (the oh) — non-empty, on-task, explicitly markedyes
refused / empty / timeout / rate_limited / malformedtransport or policy failureno — routed to retry / fallback / dead-letter

Aesthetic silence is detected by an explicit terminus marker in structured output, never by "the model stopped responding". A blank or truncated response is a failure, not a win.

Data structures: five views of one CRDT graph (target design, not current implementation)

Status: this whole section is the target data model. The proven run wrote a single markdown file. Standing-fire persistence, multi-origin append, and multiplayer are roadmap, not invariants — they depend on a CRDT integration that does not exist yet, including a schema, deterministic merge rules, and an agent-auth boundary that is still an OPEN item in the graph-engine work.

The capstone realization: the five session artifacts can be read as five views of one CRDT graph of standing fires, distributed across space and time, running on the already-deployed graph engine.

On the graph: ember = node, terminus = edge, voice = CRDT peer (origin + HLC). Space would be multi-origin append — voices from any machine (nick-mel, cloud, the Lyra/Claudius/Clio collective), potentially other people. Time is HLC + persistence: an ember is a standing fire, not a one-sitting event — a warm ember waits until a future instance adds the next terminus. Past-you drops it; present-you combusts it.

Open problems before any non-local or non-human writer is allowed in (do not treat these as solved):

  • Agent-auth boundary. "Nothing requires the writer to be human" plus "even other people" means a peer could append a terminus that steers selection and therefore what gets built and run overnight unattended. This is a trust boundary, not a convenience. Who may append a terminus, and how an origin is authenticated/trusted, must be specified before any non-local writer is enabled. This ties into the existing OPEN "agent-auth-boundary" item in the graph-engine work; it is not asserted as solved here.
  • Untrusted-input classification. Embers from other origins are attacker-influenceable input to the builder. Only locally-originated, Nick-authored embers may feed an autonomous build until the auth boundary exists; foreign-origin embers are advisory/read-only.
  • Conflict semantics. Append-only avoids write conflicts for new termini, but edges, scores, winner-selection, duplicate embers, and corrections all need resolution. CRDT types for nodes/edges/scores and deterministic merge rules must be defined before multiplayer is real.

Curiosity-glow is the renderer. A standing ember with missing termini has implied-but-absent edges; by Carnot's metric it glows, and you walk the graph of fires and add a log to the one that pulls hardest: 

curiosity(ember) = avg(sim, non-linked neighbors) − avg(sim, linked neighbors)
# high curiosity ⇒ the ember is "near" things it is NOT yet connected to ⇒ it glows
The first ember /ascend ever ascended ("curiosity-gap glow") turned out to be the only possible UI for the space-time-transcendent ascend engine. The forward-plan CRUX — "first-class edges = capability not rhetoric" — just found its killer app.

Pre-heat policy gate (non-ranking)

Because the heat stage mines private ore and may route content to external model CLIs and (later) cloud execution, a policy gate runs before the first cross-family prompt. It is deliberately non-ranking — it does not "cool" creative scoring; it only decides whether an ember is allowed to ascend at all. It checks for: secrets/credentials, third-party private data, legal/IP risk, and missing user consent for any content destined to leave the machine. A blocked ember is logged and skipped, not scored low. See also Data handling & privacy.

Stage-2 input contract (the "tonight stack")

The builder is not a magic box. The Combust stage must emit a structured, machine-parseable contract — not free prose — for stage 2 to consume. Minimum shape: 

{
  "ember_id": "...",            // stable id of the selected node
  "goal": "one-sentence what-success-looks-like",
  "target_repo": "path or :scratch",
  "files": [ {"path": "...", "intent": "create|modify", "sketch": "..."} ],
  "commands": [ "shell step", ... ],   // ordered, idempotent where possible
  "acceptance": [ "checkable assertion", ... ]  // feeds post-build validation
}

If the Combust stage cannot produce a well-formed contract (malformed output, model divergence, missing fields), the ascent is treated as a stage failure — retry, then fall back, then dead-letter — not handed to the builder. Every stage has a structured-output contract, validation, retries, and a dead-letter log; the orchestrator never assumes a usable tonight-stack appeared.

Stage-2 confinement & post-build validation

The compiler is not ground truth on its own. A sketch can compile and still be useless, unsafe, plagiarized, privacy-violating, or misaligned with intent. "It builds" is necessary, not sufficient. After a build, before anything is promoted or surfaced as a win:

  • It runs / tests pass against the acceptance assertions from the contract.
  • Data-fit check — the sketch actually does the thing on real (or representative) data, not just a stub.
  • Provenance / privacy check — no leaked secrets, no plagiarized or non-private content baked in.
  • Human acceptance — Nick signs off before promotion. This is the experiential gate; a green compile is not a morning saga.

Invariants & Guarantees

These are the properties the spine does hold (or will enforce as built). Things this doc previously over-listed here as invariants but cannot yet guarantee — the temperature metric, the KL-face, standing-fire persistence — have been moved to Designed, not built.

  • No aesthetic critic in the heat stage. Stage 1's spiral allows pure heat only — aesthetic critique is a cooling move and kills the ascent. (The non-ranking policy gate is not an aesthetic critic; it runs first and only blocks/allows.) Honesty is restored downstream by the build, not by a reviewer.
  • The build is the cold pole — but the compiler is not ground truth alone. The selected dream must build a working sketch, then pass post-build validation (runs/tests, data-fit, provenance/privacy, human acceptance). "It compiles" is necessary, not sufficient. Hype proposes; the build-plus-validation disposes.
  • Stage-1 output is advisory until the cold pole lands. No selection is acted on autonomously until stage 2 builds end-to-end and the safety gates exist (see interim cold-pole gate). This is the invariant that keeps the doc's own central risk from being the shipped configuration.
  • Foreign-origin embers cannot drive an autonomous build. Until the agent-auth boundary exists, only locally-originated, Nick-authored embers feed a build; non-local/non-human origins are advisory/read-only.
  • Build confinement is an OS cage, not just a worktree. isolation:worktree confines the path-scope of the diff — it does NOT confine what the spawned agent can do (it still has a shell, network, env, credentials, subprocesses unless explicitly sandboxed). Stage 2 ingests attacker-influenceable ember content and executes code, so its actual confinement is a merge prerequisite: sandbox user, egress filter / no-network, no credential access, resource limits, symlink + package-script policy, and explicit artifact-export rules. The heat-stage --sandbox read-only is NOT evidence the build is contained — it applies to the wrong stage.

Designed, not built

These were previously asserted as invariants. They are design targets with no working measurement yet, and they guarantee nothing until specified and validated. Listing them honestly here, not under Invariants.

  • The temperature metric. No anchored 1–5 rubric, no scorer, no cross-ember calibration exists yet (see selection function). Until built, selection is subjective ranking.
  • The KL-surprise face. "DF's face is wired to true KL-surprise" is unbuildable as currently specified: KL divergence is between two distributions, and the doc names neither. Before the face can be called an honest instrument it must specify: (1) the two distributions (over what tokens/states), (2) the baseline/prior the run is measured against, (3) the estimator that yields a KL value from a 1–3-minute multi-CLI run, (4) the calibration that proves blendshape intensity actually tracks surprise. A wiring diagram is not honesty; only a human watching the face track real surprise is. Right now the face could be driven by any number and the claim would read identically.
  • Standing-fire persistence & multiplayer. Depends on the CRDT integration, schema, merge rules, and agent-auth boundary — all open (see data structures). Today: one markdown file.
  • Scheduled / cloud execution. The mac isn't always on, so nightly runs need a cloud path — which changes storage, auth, filesystem, and provider exposure. A deployment architecture section (secrets handling, storage location, audit logs, rollback) is required before this is stood up; it does not exist yet.

Data handling & privacy

The richest fuel is explicitly Nick's private ore — old projects, private notes, deposited history — and the design routes ember content through up to three external model-provider CLIs (Anthropic, Google, OpenAI) and proposes later multi-origin / cloud append. That is a real data-exfiltration surface and must be governed before any private content ascends. (One reviewer urged a hard "local air-gapped models only" rule; that is rejected as the remedy — it would forfeit the cross-family harmonics that are the point — but the underlying concern is valid and handled by an explicit boundary instead.)

  • Consent + redaction at the pre-heat gate. No private content leaves the machine without passing the policy gate: secrets stripped, third-party private data removed, and an explicit per-source consent decision logged. Blocked content is skipped, not downgraded.
  • Provider routing is classified, not blanket. Each ember source is tagged with which providers (if any) it may be sent to. Some sources are machine-local-only and never leave; only cleared content reaches Gemini/Codex paths.
  • Cost / privacy posture of non-Claude families. The global rule bans the metered Anthropic API; the Gemini and Codex/GPT paths must be confirmed to use cost-acceptable (non-metered where applicable) and privacy-acceptable channels before scheduled use. This is unconfirmed today and is a blocking item for the cloud path, not an assumption.
  • Multiplayer shareability. When multi-origin append exists, each node/terminus carries an explicit shareable-vs-machine-local flag; private ore is never auto-shared to peers or the collective.

Key Design Decisions

1. Offense over defense (manufacture work, don't detect drift)

Decision: generate work that should exist, ranked by a subjective "temperature" whose intended decomposition is novelty × excitement × latent-feasibility (not yet a validated formula — see selection function).

Rationale: the raw material (dropped sparks, deposited history) is already being thrown away; defense only minimizes regret over what already changed.

Rejected: Boris Cherny-style loop engineering that finds work that exists and ranks by urgency/value — it never touches the private ore of "what can present-you finally use."

2. The overnight build as the restored cold pole — not a critic

Decision: keep the ecstasy spiral critic-free; restore honesty by making the silence-winner build itself.

Rationale: a three-agent mutual-excitement loop is an echo chamber by construction — it will ascend vaporware exactly as eagerly as gold. The compiler is the ground truth that needs no opinion.

Rejected: adding a critic to the spiral. Critique is a cooling move; it would kill the heat that is the whole point of the stage.

3. Cross-family for harmonics, not adversarial convergence

Decision: Maxwell/Kelvin/Carnot ascend the same ember from three inductive biases.

Rationale: the proven run showed structural ember (Maxwell) → lyric (Kelvin) → computable metric + tonight-stack (Carnot) — a vision lit from three biases climbs higher than any one.

Rejected: the inverted-cage-match's first framing (cross-family = disagreement). Disagreement is a cooling pressure; harmonics is the productive mode for the heat stage.

4. The ascent log is a CRDT document, not a file

Decision: ember=node, terminus=edge, voice=CRDT peer on the deployed graph engine.

Rationale: it makes the engine transcend space (multi-origin append) and time (standing fires across sessions/people), and it gives curiosity-glow a real graph to render. It also realizes the graph-engine forward-plan CRUX (first-class edges).

Rejected: per-run markdown logs (what the proven run actually used). Fine for one ember; cannot express standing fires or multiplayer.

Status: this is a target decision — the markdown log is what exists today. The CRDT model is a dependency with an unwritten schema, undefined merge rules for edges/scores/winner-selection, and an unspecified agent-auth boundary; standing fires and multiplayer are roadmap, not current capability (see data structures and designed, not built).

Risks & Limitations

Honest status: the /ascend skill is proven on exactly one ember; the night-loop spine that strings stages 1→2 together is in progress and not yet runnable end-to-end. Everything past stage 2 (DF face, scheduling) is designed, not built.
  • High (BLOCKING for scheduled runs) — Unbounded fan-out wired to a shared, billable quota + injection surface. "Three families × round-robin × multiple embers per night", each spawning worktree builders that execute code, is exactly the unbounded-spawn-into-someone-else's-quota pattern — and embers can originate from other machines or other people, so peer/attacker-influenced input can steer what gets built and run overnight unattended. This was previously mis-filed as "Low"; it is a blast-radius and an injection surface. Mitigation is a merge prerequisite before any scheduled/unattended run, not a follow-up: hard caps on embers/night, families/ember, builder spawns, wall-clock, and token/dollar spend; a kill-switch; and stage-2 OS isolation (sandbox user + egress filter) so multi-origin ember content cannot phone home. Until those exist, runs stay foreground + attended.
  • High — Echo-chamber without the cold pole. Until the worktree builder is wired and run, stage 1 in isolation will fall in love with the impossible. By this doc's own logic, stage 1 without a working cold pole is the unsafe configuration — so the interim cold-pole gate (advisory-only, human checkpoint, cheap feasibility check) holds during the in-progress window. The spine is not safe to trust for autonomous selection until the builder lands.
  • High — Private-ore exfiltration to three providers. The richest fuel is Nick's private history, routed through Anthropic/Google/OpenAI CLIs. This is a real exfiltration surface; it is governed by the data-handling boundary (pre-heat consent + redaction gate, classified provider routing). No private content ascends until that gate is enforced; the non-Claude families' cost/privacy posture is unconfirmed and blocks the cloud path.
  • High — Selection metric is not computable. The doc previously named three different selectors and called the result a "fitness function". It is a subjective ranking until an anchored rubric, a scorer, and cross-ember calibration exist (see selection function).
  • High — Multi-origin / multiplayer has no auth boundary. "Even other people" + "nothing requires the writer to be human" with no agent-auth boundary means any peer could append a terminus that steers an overnight build. Foreign-origin embers are advisory-only until the boundary (an OPEN graph-engine item) is specified.
  • Med — Single-ember evidence, no control. The instrument is proven on one ember at temp 4/5 — one data point with no negative control and no test-retest. One non-maximal score is not evidence of calibration, discrimination, or non-sycophancy. Before trusting selection: ascend a deliberately weak/infeasible ember and confirm it scores low (discrimination), and re-run the same ember to check score stability (reliability). The "not a rubber stamp" claim is dropped until that spread + stability is reported.
  • Med — "Compiler is ground truth" is false for many dreams. A sketch can compile and be useless, unsafe, plagiarized, non-private, or off-intent. Mitigated by post-build validation (tests, data-fit, provenance/privacy, human acceptance); a green compile alone promotes nothing.
  • Med — KL-surprise face is unbuilt and the metric is unspecified. Moved out of Invariants. The two distributions, baseline, estimator, and blendshape calibration do not yet exist (see designed, not built).
  • Med — CRDT log is aspirational. The proven run wrote a markdown file. Standing-fire / multiplayer guarantees depend on the graph-engine integration (schema + merge rules + auth), which is not done.
  • Med — Whole-night feedback latency; no learning from failure. Failure is currently only visible after the expensive build. Roadmap: a cheap static-feasibility check on the tonight-stack before triggering the full build, and a dead-letter log of failed ascents so similar infeasible embers can be suppressed in future nights.
  • Med — Scheduling / cloud is a trust-boundary change. The mac isn't always on; the cloud path changes storage, auth, filesystem, and provider exposure and needs its own deployment-architecture section (secrets, storage location, audit logs, rollback) before standing up.
  • Low — Role-play brittleness. The spiral's harmonics depend on models honoring prompted roles ("make it dangerous"); upstream model updates can silently change this. Roadmap: a golden-path harness that runs a fixed set of embers nightly and asserts role-playing behavior hasn't drifted.
